Best Books to Read

Classic Fiction

  • "To Kill a Mockingbird" by Harper Lee – A powerful exploration of race and morality.
  • "1984" by George Orwell – A dystopian masterpiece about totalitarianism.
  • "Pride and Prejudice" by Jane Austen – A classic romance with witty social commentary.
  • "The Great Gatsby" by F. Scott Fitzgerald – A look at the American Dream in the Jazz Age.

Science Fiction & Fantasy

  • "Dune" by Frank Herbert – A sweeping saga on politics, religion, and power.
  • "The Lord of the Rings" by J.R.R. Tolkien – An epic journey through Middle-earth.
  • "Neuromancer" by William Gibson – A foundational cyberpunk novel.
  • "Foundation" by Isaac Asimov – A science fiction classic about the preservation of knowledge.

Non-Fiction

  • "Sapiens: A Brief History of Humankind" by Yuval Noah Harari – A fascinating look at human evolution and society.
  • "Educated" by Tara Westover – A memoir of overcoming a restrictive upbringing through education.
  • "The Power of Habit" by Charles Duhigg – Insights into the science behind habits.
  • "Becoming" by Michelle Obama – The former First Lady’s inspiring memoir.

Self-Help & Personal Development

  • "Atomic Habits" by James Clear – A guide to making small, effective changes.
  • "The Subtle Art of Not Giving a F*ck" by Mark Manson – A unique approach to self-help.
  • "How to Win Friends and Influence People" by Dale Carnegie – Timeless advice on personal effectiveness.
  • "The Power of Now" by Eckhart Tolle – A guide to mindfulness and presence.

Thrillers & Mystery

  • "Gone Girl" by Gillian Flynn – A psychological thriller with unexpected twists.
  • "The Girl with the Dragon Tattoo" by Stieg Larsson – A gripping mystery novel.
  • "Sherlock Holmes" series by Arthur Conan Doyle – Classic detective stories.
  • "Big Little Lies" by Liane Moriarty – A story of friendship, secrets, and lies.

Modern Literature

  • "The Road" by Cormac McCarthy – A haunting tale of survival and fatherhood.
  • "The Kite Runner" by Khaled Hosseini – A story of friendship and redemption in Afghanistan.
  • "Life of Pi" by Yann Martel – A philosophical adventure with themes of faith.
  • "Where the Crawdads Sing" by Delia Owens – A mystery set in the marshlands of North Carolina.
